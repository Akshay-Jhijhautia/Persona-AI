# Persona Data Collection

## Overview

The persona definitions in this project were created from manual observations of publicly available material associated with Hitesh Choudhary and Piyush Garg.

The goal was not to reproduce a person exactly. The goal was to identify broad, recognizable teaching characteristics that could guide an educational AI simulation.

## Public Sources

Observations were drawn from public sources such as:

- Official websites and public profile pages
- Public YouTube videos and tutorials
- Conference talks, interviews, and podcasts
- Public blog posts and written material
- Public social media presence

No private accounts, private messages, confidential documents, paid datasets, or personally obtained information were used.

## Collection Method

No automated scraping pipeline was required for version 1. The collection process was manual:

1. Review representative public teaching content.
2. Note repeated tone, vocabulary, explanation patterns, and audience level.
3. Identify how technical concepts are introduced and simplified.
4. Record commonly observed phrases without treating them as mandatory scripts.
5. Convert the observations into structured persona guidance.

The final guidance is organized into:

- Persona traits
- Tone and language preferences
- Teaching style
- Common conversational phrases
- Recommended behaviors
- Do and don't rules

## Privacy and Limitations

Only public-facing teaching behavior was considered. The project does not infer private beliefs, personal data, or off-camera behavior.

The personas are simplified prompt-based interpretations. They may not capture the full range or current style of the real individuals, and generated responses should not be presented as authentic statements from them.
